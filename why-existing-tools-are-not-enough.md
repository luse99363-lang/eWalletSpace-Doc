# Why Existing Tools Are Not Enough

After discovering eWalletSpace, many people ask the same question:

Why do I need another platform if I already use GMGN, DexScreener, or Arkham?

It's a logical question.

All of these tools have long become part of the everyday workflow of on-chain traders and do an excellent job at what they are designed to do.

The problem is that they solve a different task.

### What Existing Services Can Do

If you need to:

* View a token chart;
* Check liquidity;
* Open the list of recent trades;
* Find a token contract;
* Analyze a specific wallet;

then existing services do an excellent job. We use them ourselves every day. eWalletSpace does not try to replace GMGN, DexScreener, DEXTools, or Arkham.

On the contrary, these services complement each other perfectly.

### But Then Another Problem Appears

Imagine a typical situation. You find an interesting token. For example, you discovered it on GMGN after a successful Launchpad migration. Or you noticed an attractive chart on DexScreener. An obvious question arises:

Who bought this token before everyone else? You open the list of buyers. In front of you are hundreds, and sometimes thousands, of wallets. And this is where the most difficult part begins.

### Manual Analysis

Now you need to open every wallet individually.

Check:

* What it traded before;
* How successfully it traded;
* Which tokens it bought;
* How consistently it found strong projects;
* Whether there are scam trades in its history;
* Whether its success was random or consistent.

Even if it takes only one minute to review a single wallet, analyzing a popular token can easily turn into several hours of work.

And in most cases, you will find that the vast majority of those wallets are not interesting to you at all.

### Why This Happens

Because existing services display data. But they do very little to help filter it. They answer the question:

What happened?

But they hardly help answer the question:

Who is actually worth paying attention to?

That is why finding strong traders is still largely a manual process.

### The eWalletSpace Approach

We decided to look at this problem from a different perspective.

Instead of making users analyze every discovered buyer themselves, the platform does most of that work in advance.

For every wallet, the main trading metrics are calculated.

The user immediately sees:

* ROI;
* PnL;
* Win Rate;
* Number of Trades;
* Trading Volume;
* Average Potential;
* Trading History.

All of this is available before opening the wallet profile.

### The Main Advantage - Speed

Imagine two scenarios.

#### Without eWalletSpace You find a token.

You open the list of buyers. You manually check dozens or hundreds of addresses. An hour later, you find one interesting trader.

#### With eWalletSpace You find a token.

You paste its contract address into the search. Within a few seconds, you receive a list of wallets that have already been analyzed.

You sort them by Potential. You open several of the most interesting profiles. Within a few minutes, you already understand whether this token has traders worth saving or copying.

The difference is not the amount of information. The difference is the amount of time required to get the result.

### Why Searching by Token Turned Out to Be the Most Effective Approach

After working with the platform, we came to a simple conclusion. The best wallets are rarely found through random searches across the entire market. Much more often, they are found through good tokens.

That is why the main workflow looks like this:

1. Find an interesting token.
2. Open the list of all traders.
3. Sort the wallets.
4. Find interesting patterns.
5. Move on to the next tokens traded by those wallets.
6. Repeat the process.

This gradually builds your own database of strong wallets. This approach is the foundation of the eWalletSpace philosophy.

### Why We Created the Potential Metric

During development, we encountered another problem. Almost every analytics service evaluates the final result of a trade.

They show:

* How much the trader earned;
* Their ROI;
* Their Win Rate.

However, these metrics say nothing about the quality of the entry itself. Imagine two traders. Both bought the same token. After they bought it, the token increased by 3000%. The first trader sold almost at the very top.

The second trader panicked and exited five minutes later with a small profit. Their ROI would be completely different. But both of them found a strong project before the market did. We believe that this ability is the most valuable. That is why eWalletSpace introduced the Potential metric.

It evaluates the quality of the entry, not the quality of the exit. Because of this, it becomes possible to find traders who cannot be identified by looking only at ROI or PnL.

### eWalletSpace Does Not Replace Existing Services

It is important to understand one thing. After you start using eWalletSpace, you do not need to stop using GMGN or DexScreener. Quite the opposite.

The best results are achieved when the tools work together.

A typical workflow might look like this:

1. Find an interesting token on GMGN or another discovery platform.
2. Open that token in eWalletSpace.
3. Find strong traders.
4. Go back to the charts and study their trades.
5. Make your own decision.

That is why we do not describe eWalletSpace as a replacement for existing services. We see it as the next stage of analysis.

### Chapter Summary

Most analytics platforms help you analyze tokens and individual wallets. eWalletSpace helps you find traders. That is the main difference. We do not aim to display more data.

We aim to shorten the path from discovering an interesting token to finding a wallet that is truly worth your attention.
